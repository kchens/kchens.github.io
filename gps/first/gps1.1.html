Kevin and Mike's GPS Pairing Page

Why Pairing is important to DBC...

Pairing is important to DBC because it helps you learn to code better, learn to work with others, and prepares you for real-world jobs in web development. Pair programming enables boots to practice what they learn in Engineering Empathy. Furthermore, collaboration helps with spontaneous learning and coming up with creative solutions to problems.

What being "The Driver" means...

    Kevin

  • The driver types.
  • The driver shares what she/he is going to do with the navigator, before she/he does it.

    Michael

  • The driver makes it happen.
  • The driver checks in periodically with the navigator making sure they are on track.

What being "The Navigator" means...

    Kevin

  • The navigator only thinks and talks -- no typing.
  • The navigator is the there if the driver needs advice, big or small.

    Michael

  • The navigator has the big picture in mind.
  • The navigator chimes in if she/he see the driver going down "the rabbit hole".

How today's pairing went...

Today's pairing went very well, despite some technical difficulties and it being our first guided pairing session. While it was a little nerve-racking pairing in-front of the guide, Kevin and Mike both feel the communication and chemistry was great. It helped that, from the start, we were open about our ideas on how pairing should play out. By doing so, Kevin and Mike were able to work flexibly and produce this "sweet" web page.
