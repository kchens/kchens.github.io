    • Relative, absolute, and fixed positioning may sound like nonsense to you. As words, you're probably knowledgeable about the meaning, but, when it comes to programming CSS, they all mean the same thing to you: nonsense. Well, if you're a beginner, no fear! I'm here to illuminate the difference.

    magical scrapbook

    To talk about each, we first have to lay some ground rules. First, we have to talk about static positioning - the default positioning value of anything you see on a webpage. Second, we need an analogy; imagine a scrapbook!

    Now, this is a magical scrapbook. And, one of the magical rules it has is that you must place each and every picture in the top left corner -- no "if"s, "and"s, or "but"s.

    static positioning

    In placing your first picture into the scrapbook that means you'll have to abide by the scrapbook's "default" rules. So, you put that picture of the cake you baked in the top left. This default positioning has another name: static positioning. (To say another way, there is no positioning)

    relative positioning

    What if you would like to move forgo the magical scrapbooks crummy rules? Well, you can call upon the powers of relative positioning. By doing so in the stylesheet, you can then move your picture anywhere you want to! You just need to specify how far the picture is from the top, right, bottom, and left sides of the scrapbook page.

    fixed positioning

    Now, as often happens with scrapbooks, you run out of space. But instead of going to the next page, this is a magical scrapbook. Instead of flipping left-to-right, this scrapbook is a vertical scroll -- like a Chinese painting or pirate's treasure map.

    You can use this to your benefit. As you care a lot about your mom (we all do), you'll want to keep your mom's picture even with your eyes even as you unscroll. Fixed positioning does this. But, where to keep your mom's picture? Closer to the top so she's know's she's #1? Or at the bottom because she's always got your "back"? To the left? Right? Well, you can specify that too with proper margin and padding.

    absolute positioning

    But let's be honest here, your mom can't be with you all the time. And you probably don't want her staring you down shamefully as you manage your finances...or what's left of it. Not a problem! Absolute positioning makes it so that she's only in the picture when you're looking at the OKCupid portion of the scroll. With absolute positioning, your mom will only stay within the OKCupid part of the page -- similar to how fixed positioned picture stay in the exact same place inside the browser. You can also specify the top, right, bottom, and left margins the absolutely-positioned picture is within the OKCupid box.
